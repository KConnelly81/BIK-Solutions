## Floor Protection Boards — Application & Care

### Application
1. Ensure the floor surface is clean, dry and free from debris before laying boards.
2. Place boards flat on the floor with edges butting together.
3. Secure joins with double-sided floor tape (available separately) to prevent movement and tripping hazards.
4. For stairways, cut boards to fit each tread using a utility knife or circular saw.

### Care during use
- Boards can be walked on and used as a traffic surface immediately.
- Avoid dragging heavy sharp objects across the surface.
- If a board becomes saturated with water or liquid, remove and allow to dry before reusing.
- Do not use boards on wet concrete — wait until the surface is fully cured.

### Removal
1. Remove double-sided tape from joins before lifting boards.
2. Stack and store flat to prevent warping.
3. Wipe down with a damp cloth to remove construction dust before storing.

### Reuse
Boards can be reused across multiple jobs. Inspect before each use for splits, heavy soiling or delamination. Boards that have lost structural integrity should be disposed of.

## Self-Adhesive Films (Floor & Carpet) — Application & Care

### Surface preparation
- Surface must be clean, dry and dust-free. Lay film on dirty floors and the adhesive will pick up debris, reducing adhesion.
- For polished or waxed timber, test a small corner first to confirm clean removal before full application.

### Application
1. Start at one end of the room. Peel back approximately 500mm of backing paper.
2. Lay the exposed film onto the surface and smooth from the centre outward to remove air bubbles.
3. Walk along the roll, peeling and pressing as you go.
4. Cut at doorways and edges with a utility knife.
5. At joins, overlap by at least 50mm or use painters tape to seal the gap.

### During use
- Foot traffic is fine from application. Light vehicle traffic (e.g., site trolleys) is acceptable for heavy-duty films.
- If a section lifts, re-press the edge or apply a strip of painters tape to hold.
- Do not apply to floors that have been freshly painted or coated in the last 7 days.

### Removal
- Remove within the specified protection period (30 days for floor films; 90 days for window film).
- Peel back slowly at a low angle — do not pull upward.
- If adhesive residue remains, apply a small amount of mineral turpentine on a cloth and wipe. Test on a small area first.

## Window & Glass Protection Film — Application & Care

### Application
1. Clean the glass surface thoroughly — any dirt under the film will be visible.
2. Peel and apply the same way as floor film, pressing firmly at edges.
3. Use a squeegee or flat edge (e.g., credit card) to smooth bubbles from the centre outward.
4. Seal edges with painters tape if the film will be exposed to water (e.g., external windows in rain).

### Removal
- Remove within 90 days.
- Score the edge with a knife, then peel slowly.
- If residue remains, use mineral turpentine or a glass-safe adhesive remover.

## Dust Barrier Film (Poly Sheeting) — Application & Care

### Building a dust enclosure
1. Measure and cut sheeting to the required height (floor to ceiling, plus 200mm floor seal).
2. Attach to walls at the top with duct tape. Fold 200mm to the floor and secure with double-sided floor tape or duct tape.
3. Overlap joining sheets by at least 300mm and tape the full join with duct tape.
4. Fit a zipper door kit at the primary entry point (see below).
5. Check all joins and seals before beginning dusty work.

### Zipper Door Kit — Application
1. Clean the door surround (frame) thoroughly.
2. Peel the adhesive backing from the frame strips and press firmly around the door opening.
3. Attach the zipper barrier panel to the frame strips.
4. Test the zipper closes fully.
5. Ensure the bottom of the barrier is sealed to the floor with duct or floor tape.

### Tear-down
- Score tape before pulling poly sheeting from walls to avoid damaging paint.
- Roll up used sheeting carefully to contain dust — do not shake or drag.

## Tape — Application Tips

### Painters Tape
- Apply to clean, dry surfaces for best adhesion and clean removal.
- Press the edge firmly with your finger or a squeegee tool.
- Remove tape while paint is still slightly wet (before fully cured) for the cleanest line.
- Remove at a 45-degree angle, pulling slowly away from the masked surface.

### Double-Sided Floor Tape
- Clean both surfaces before applying.
- Press firmly for at least 10 seconds per section.
- To remove: score the edge with a knife and peel slowly. Use mineral turpentine for adhesive residue.

### Duct Tape
- Apply to clean surfaces for maximum adhesion.
- Press firmly along the full length of the tape.
- For dust barrier sealing, overlap tape runs by at least 20mm and press all edges firmly.
