## Tool Register

### Tool: Progress Claim
**Regulatory context:** Security of payment legislation varies by state. In Queensland, the BIF Act 2017 replaced the Building and Construction Industry Payments Act 2004. Claims must be made on or after a reference date (BIF Act s.68). Head contractor claims in Queensland may require a supporting statement (BIF Act s.77). Subcontractors claiming from a head contractor must comply with subcontract payment claim requirements.  
**What BIK provides:** A structured draft payment claim document. Not a payment claim system. Does not calculate reference dates. Does not assess whether the claim meets the formal requirements of the applicable Act.  
**What BIK does NOT provide:** Compliance advice; legal assessment of whether the claim satisfies the applicable Act; calculation of payment schedule response deadlines.  
**Professional review status:** Pending — construction lawyer required to validate legislative accuracy across all states and confirm supporting statement guidance is appropriate.  
**Release status:** Controlled Beta

---

### Tool: SWMS — Safe Work Method Statement
**Regulatory context:** High-risk construction work (HRPW) as defined in the Work Health and Safety Regulation 2011 (Qld) Schedule 1, division 6 (reg. 291) requires a SWMS before work commences. The SWMS must be site-specific, prepared through consultation with workers (reg. 296), and reviewed by a competent person. It must be kept at the workplace and reviewed when conditions change (reg. 299).  
**What BIK provides:** A structured form to capture and document SWMS information. A starting point for the document, not a compliant document.  
**What BIK does NOT provide:** Verification that the identified hazards are complete; verification that the control measures are adequate; confirmation of compliance; a substitute for worker consultation; a substitute for competent-person review; a substitute for implementation and monitoring.  
**AI Writer restriction:** The controlMeasures field must not use AI Writer to generate control measures that are then presented as site-adequate without review. This is a known risk. AI Writer is currently enabled on this field — this must be addressed before release.  
**Professional review status:** HOLD — WHS professional review required before any release.  
**Release status:** Professional Review in Progress — DO NOT RELEASE

**Professional review status:** Not required.

### Tool: Payment Reminder
**Regulatory context:** A payment reminder letter does not itself create legal rights. Level 3 (final notice) copy references intent to suspend works — users must verify they have a contractual right to suspend before doing so. A payment reminder is not a formal statutory demand under the Corporations Act 2001.  
**What BIK provides:** A professional letter requesting payment. Three escalation levels.  
**Professional review status:** Partial — Level 3 copy should be reviewed by a construction lawyer.  
**Release status:** Controlled Beta

---

### Tool: Practical Completion Notice
**Regulatory context:** Practical completion is a contractual concept. The contract defines what constitutes practical completion and the notice requirements. Requirements differ between residential (QBCC contract) and commercial (AS4000) contracts. An occupancy permit may also be required before handover is legally permissible.  
**What BIK provides:** A structured draft notice of practical completion.  
**Professional review status:** Not required for initial controlled release.  
**Release status:** Controlled Beta

---

### Tool: Notice to Show Cause
**Regulatory context:** Most standard form contracts (AS4000 cl. 39, HIA, ABIC) require a show cause notice before termination. The form, content, and notice period vary by contract. Issuing a notice without a genuine contractual basis may itself constitute a breach or repudiation.  
**What BIK provides:** A structured draft notice to show cause. Not contract-specific.  
**What BIK does NOT provide:** Legal advice on whether a show cause notice is appropriate in the circumstances; verification that the notice meets the requirements of the applicable contract; advice on how to serve the notice.  
**Professional review status:** HOLD — construction lawyer required.  
**Release status:** Professional Review in Progress — DO NOT RELEASE

---

### Tool: Contract Termination Notice
**Regulatory context:** Termination rights are strictly contract-specific. Wrongful termination — i.e. purporting to terminate without a valid contractual right — can itself constitute repudiation of the contract and expose the terminating party to liability for the other party's losses. A termination notice must be correct in content, form, and service.  
**What BIK provides:** A structured draft termination notice.  
**Risk level:** EXTREME — highest-risk document in the suite.  
**Professional review status:** HOLD — construction lawyer required before any release.  
**Release status:** Professional Review in Progress — DO NOT RELEASE

### Tool: Subcontractor Agreement
**Regulatory context:** The BIF Act 2017 (Qld) imposes retention trust obligations on principal contractors who retain more than 1% of a subcontract amount exceeding $1M. Subcontracts above certain thresholds may require specific terms. The agreement does not include dispute resolution, intellectual property, or assignment clauses that may be required for complex engagements.  
**What BIK provides:** A simple subcontract purchase order / agreement for straightforward trade subcontracts.  
**What BIK does NOT provide:** A fully comprehensive subcontract; advice on whether the generated agreement is appropriate for the specific engagement; advice on BIF Act retention trust obligations.  
**Professional review status:** HOLD — construction lawyer required.  
**Release status:** Professional Review in Progress — DO NOT RELEASE

### Tool: Incident Report
**Regulatory context:** This is a RECORD-KEEPING tool only. Serious workplace incidents (fatality, serious injury, dangerous occurrence) trigger a mandatory immediate notification obligation to the relevant WHS regulator (WHS Act s.38). In Queensland, this is WorkSafe Queensland (1300 362 128). This tool does not satisfy the statutory notification obligation. The user must notify the regulator immediately and separately.  
**What BIK provides:** A structured incident record for internal documentation purposes.  
**Professional review status:** Not required for release, but tool description and in-tool notice must clearly state the statutory notification obligation.  
**Release status:** Controlled Beta — with mandatory statutory notification warning

---

### Tool: Extension of Time Claim
**Regulatory context:** EOT entitlements are contract-specific. Most construction contracts (AS4000 cl. 35, HIA, QBCC) impose strict notice requirements — failure to give notice within the specified time may extinguish the entitlement. The tool does not calculate notice deadlines.  
**Release status:** Controlled Beta
