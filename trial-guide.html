Founding Builder Program

Your first 7 days with BIK

A day-by-day plan for getting real value out of BIK during the Beta. Follow it at your own pace — the order matters less than doing all seven.

How to use this guide: Each day has a clear task that takes 5–15 minutes. Some tasks use BIK directly on a live job — others are about learning how the tool works. By the end of the week, you'll have used BIK on a real project and sent your first feedback.

You don't have to do one a day — work through them as your schedule allows. The goal is to get comfortable before the end of your first week.

Day 1
Set up your profile and explore the dashboard
10 min

Your first job is to set up your Builder Profile — the business details that auto-fill into every document. Once this is done, you'll never retype your ABN or licence number again.

  • Click Profile on the dashboard (top right).
  • Enter your business name, ABN, QBCC or state licence number, phone, email, and business address.
  • Add the name of the person who signs documents.
  • Click Save profile.
  • Explore the dashboard — note the tool cards, project list, and health indicators.
Tip: Your profile is stored on this device only. Nothing is sent to a server. If you use BIK on a second device, set up the profile there too.

→ Go to Dashboard
Day 2
Create your first real project
5 min

Add one of your current or upcoming jobs as a project in BIK. You'll see how the health dashboard tracks activity for it.

  • Click New Project on the dashboard.
  • Enter the project name (e.g. "Jones Renovation — Chapel Hill").
  • Add the client name, site address, and contract value if you have it.
  • Set status to Active and click Save project.
Tip: Don't worry about filling in every field. A name and status is enough — you can add detail later. The project health indicator updates as you add documents to the project.

→ Add a project
Day 3
Generate a Variation Notice for a live job
10 min

If you have a scope change on any current job, use BIK to document it today. This is the highest-value task in the guide — a variation notice prevents a dispute before it starts.

  • Open the Variation Notice tool from the dashboard.
  • Select the project you created on Day 2 (or type the project details).
  • Describe the scope change, cost impact, and time extension.
  • Click Generate document and review the output.
  • Use Save PDF to download it, or Email to send to the client.
No live variation right now? Use the demo project (Smith Residence) as practice — it already has variation data loaded. Generate the document and see what it looks like.

→ Open Variation Notice
Day 4
Write a Site Diary entry
5 min

The Site Diary is your daily record — it's your best protection in a dispute. Try writing one today's entry to get comfortable with the format.

  • Open the Site Diary tool from the dashboard.
  • Select your project and today's date.
  • Note the weather, workforce, and works completed today (even brief notes are fine).
  • Record any instructions received, materials delivered, or delays.
  • Generate the document and save a PDF.
Habit tip: The goal is to make site diary entries a 3-minute end-of-day habit. Brief, factual, and consistent. Courts treat contemporaneous records as more reliable than later recollections.

→ Open Site Diary
Day 5
Try the AI Writer
10 min

The AI Writer rewrites your rough notes into professional, clear language — without adding facts or changing your meaning. It's useful for defect descriptions, scope explanations, and formal notices.

  • Open any tool (try Variation Notice or Defect Report).
  • Click AI Writer in the top bar and enter your Anthropic API key (see setup guide).
  • Fill in a field with rough notes — something like "cracked tile, third row from door".
  • Click the AI Writer button next to the field and choose a rewrite mode.
  • Review the output — AI rewrites, but never invents facts.
Cost note: API usage is billed to your Anthropic account. At current rates, 100 rewrites costs approximately $0.30–$0.60. See the AI Usage Notice for details.

→ AI Writer setup guide
Day 6
Explore a second tool relevant to your work
10 min

Pick one of the tools below that's most relevant to what you're working on right now, and generate a document using a current or recent job.

  • Quote Builder — for a new enquiry or tender. Try building a quote with line items.
  • Progress Claim — if you're at a claim milestone. Note: verify your state's SOP legislation before serving.
  • Defect Report — if you've just completed an inspection or handover.
  • Toolbox Talk — if you have site workers. Record a safety briefing for your records.
  • EOT Claim — if you're dealing with a delay. Issue a claim before the contract notice deadline.
Why this matters: The more tools you use, the more you'll see how they connect — a variation leads to an EOT claim, a defect report leads to a handover checklist. Each tool links to the next.

→ Browse all tools
Day 7
Send your first piece of feedback
5 min

This is the most important task of the week for the Founding Builder Program. Your feedback directly shapes what gets built next.

  • Look for the Beta button at the bottom right of any page.
  • Choose Bug Report, Feature Request, or General Feedback.
  • Tell us: what worked, what didn't, what you'd change, what you wish BIK did.
  • Include how much time you think BIK saved you this week (even a rough estimate).
  • Hit Submit — it takes about 30 seconds.
What we're looking for: We want honest feedback — not praise. If something confused you, frustrated you, or didn't work as expected, that's the most valuable thing you can tell us. We read and respond to every submission.
Week 1 checklist
  • Builder profile set up and saved
  • At least one real project created
  • Variation Notice generated for a live or demo job
  • Site Diary entry generated
  • AI Writer set up and tested
  • Second tool explored with real data
  • First feedback submitted via Beta button
