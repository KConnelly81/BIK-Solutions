Release Notes

BIK Business Toolkit

Version 0.11.1 Beta 17 July 2026 — Site Sign-In & Workforce Register

Sprint 5.2 takes Site Sign-In & Workforce Register from functional prototype to live-ready beta. A real builder and real contractors can now check in and out on a worksite in under 20 seconds — no app required. Builders get a live view of who is on site, a full audit trail of all edits, and a printable attendance record.

Site Sign-In & Workforce Register
  • ● Secure QR check-in tokens — QR sign-in URLs now use 128-bit random public tokens (e.g. checkin.html?t=a3f...) instead of internal project IDs. Tokens cannot be used to enumerate or guess other projects.
  • ● Returning worker detection — workers who scan the QR code again while already signed in see an "Already Signed In" screen with their check-in time and a direct checkout button. Prevents duplicate records without requiring the builder to intervene.
  • ● Manual checkout lookup — workers who lost their success-page link can check out by entering their name and mobile number at checkout.html. Multiple matches show a project + time selection screen for confirmation.
  • ● Edit and correction workflow — builders can correct any attendance record from the dashboard. Every edit requires a reason (data entry error, builder confirmed with worker, etc.) and is recorded in a full audit log. The audit log is accessible via the History button on each record card.
  • ● Break minutes — builders can enter unpaid break time (in minutes) against any record. Break minutes are deducted from recorded site hours and shown in exports.
  • ● Soft delete (void) — builders can void a duplicate or erroneous record with a reason. Voided records are preserved with full audit trail and excluded from all totals and reports.
  • ● Forgotten checkout flag — records open for more than 18 hours are flagged as "Checkout Required" (amber) and excluded from the site hours total. The dashboard shows an alert and the project strip shows a warning. Records are never auto-closed.
  • ● Emergency Roll Call — builders can open a roll call modal at any time showing only workers currently on site with a count and a print button. Designed for emergency musters and WHS compliance.
  • ● Print QR Sign — generates a professional A4 printable sign with project name, site address, QR code, and step-by-step instructions for workers. Accessible from both the project page and the QR modal.
  • ● "Recorded Site Hours" terminology — all references to "labour hours" replaced with "Recorded Site Hours" throughout the dashboard, exports, and reports. A tooltip clarifies: "not automatically approved payroll or billable hours".
  • ● Updated CSV exports — daily attendance and workforce register CSVs now include: Break (min), Status, Corrected indicator, and a BOM prefix for correct Excel opening. Column header "Recorded Site Hrs" replaces "Labour Hours".
  • ● Privacy notice on check-in form — workers see a clear notice before submitting: data is stored on the builder's device only, not transmitted to external servers.
  • ● Standardised 6 worker types — Employee, Subcontractor, Contractor, Labour Hire, Visitor, Inspector, Supplier, Other. Company field required for subcontractor/contractor/supplier/inspector. Trade required for subcontractor/contractor.
  • ● Demo data for testing — builders can load a set of demo attendance records from the Reports tab, then remove them cleanly without affecting real records. Demo records are labelled and excluded from real totals while present.
  • ● Dashboard smart actions updated — attendance alerts now use the v2 data model: "Checkout Required" (>18h workers), visitors still on site, and "No attendance today" for projects with past records. Each links directly to the relevant project attendance page.
  • ● Project page attendance strip — the strip on the project page now shows: workers on site (green), checkout required warning (amber), recorded site hours today, and a Print QR Sign button.
  • ● Schema v2 migration — existing attendance records are automatically migrated to v2 on first load. New fields (breakMinutes, status, auditLog, voidReason) are added non-destructively. No data loss.
Known limitations in this release
  • All attendance data is stored in the builder's browser (localStorage). Data is not shared between devices. If the builder clears browser storage, attendance records will be lost — cloud sync is planned for a future sprint.
  • Recorded site hours are not verified hours. They are calculated from time in, time out, and break minutes entered on the day. They are not approved payroll or billable hours and should not be used as such without independent verification.
  • GPS co-ordinates are captured at check-in on supported devices. GPS accuracy varies and is not a verified attendance mechanism on its own.
  • The workforce register records all workers who have checked in via the QR code. It does not record workers who were not on the QR check-in system.
Version 0.11 Beta 16 July 2026 — Founding Builder Experience

Sprint 5.1 is focused entirely on the first-week experience for Founding Builders. No new document generators — all effort went into onboarding, value demonstration, and the connected workflow. The goal: a builder who has never seen BIK can receive a single email, open the dashboard, and understand the full value of the product within five minutes — without any training.

New in this release
  • ● Guided onboarding wizard — 3-step setup modal for new users. Step 1: business profile (name, builder type, state, ABN, licence, GST, existing software). Step 2: create first project. Step 3: celebration with CTA to generate first document. Includes confetti animation, back navigation, and skip option. Profile auto-fills after step 1 so every subsequent document is pre-populated.
  • ● Founding Builder program page (founding-builder.html) — dedicated welcome page explaining the Founding Builder program, founder message, benefits (early access, locked pricing, roadmap voting, direct team access), and live value metrics (documents created, hours saved, estimated cost saved).
  • ● Quick Start Guide (quick-start.html) — step-by-step onboarding guide covering profile setup, first project, first document, tool selection reference, and the feedback commitment. Designed for a builder who receives one email and opens BIK independently.
  • ● FAQ page (faq.html) — comprehensive answers covering getting started, privacy, AI Writer, tools, documents, and Beta program questions.
  • ● Today's Priorities panel — new dashboard section showing critical and warning alerts across all active projects in a single prioritised list. Each priority row has a one-click action button so the most important task is always one click away.
  • ● Value Metrics bar — live display of documents created, admin hours saved, and estimated cost saved. Calculated using per-document-type time estimates at $75/hr. Transparent methodology documented in the Founding Builder page and FAQ.
  • ● Founding Builder banner — dismissible banner on the dashboard for new users, linking to the Founding Builder program page. Sets context for the Beta relationship before the builder opens any tools.
  • ● Richer demo project — "Smith Residence — Double Storey Addition" seeded with 15 documents across 8 tool types, with correct approval states and contract values. The demo project now shows a working Running Contract Value ($489,800 excl. GST: base quote $485k + approved $4.8k kitchen variation), an active pending variation, a missing site diary alert, and a suggested next action. First-time visitors see a fully operational dashboard within seconds.
  • ● Feedback email fallback — feedback submitted via the Beta button now opens a pre-filled mailto link so the feedback actually reaches BIK, not just localStorage. Previously, feedback was stored locally only with no delivery mechanism.
  • ● Star rating hover fixed — the star rating in the feedback widget now correctly highlights all stars up to the hovered star (was broken due to CSS ~ sibling selector limitation).
  • ● Health engine expanded — all 16 tools now registered in the health engine and project TOOL_META. Previously, 11 Phase 2 tools were invisible to project health analysis and the project tool history panel.
Known issues in this release
  • Demo data (Smith Residence) uses placeholder document body text rather than fully generated documents. Opening a document from History will show form fields but not a rendered preview — this is expected in the Beta.
  • The onboarding wizard's project creation uses a direct localStorage write as a fallback if projectStore.create() is unavailable. This is a minor timing issue on first load and will be resolved in v0.12.
  • Value metrics are estimates only. The $75/hr admin rate and per-document time estimates are conservative and clearly labelled as estimates. Actual savings depend on your existing workflow and document complexity.
  • The Founding Builder banner and Value Metrics bar are not yet visible on mobile screen widths below 380px due to flex wrapping — to be addressed in v0.12 polish pass.

Version 0.10 Beta 16 July 2026 — AI Project Health Dashboard

Sprint 4 replaces the static project list with an AI Project Health Dashboard — a proactive, rules-based monitoring system that watches every project and surfaces the next best action without the builder having to search for problems.

New in this release
  • ● Project Health Dashboard — each project gets a health card showing its status, contract value, active alerts, and a suggested next action. Cards are colour-coded: green (healthy), amber (needs attention), red (critical).
  • ● Health Engine — deterministic rules engine (health-engine.js) evaluates every project against its document records. Architecture is future-ready for AI recommendations — the rules engine output shape is the stable contract; swapping to a model endpoint requires no UI changes.
  • ● Running Contract Value — automatically calculates original contract (approved quote) + approved variations = current contract total, with GST estimate. Updates in real time as documents are approved.
  • ● Smart alerts with one-click actions — six alert categories: pending approval (overdue), approval awaiting, missing site diary, quote expiry, no quote on file, quote not yet sent. Every alert has a direct action button: Send Reminder, Create Site Diary, Review Quote, Create Quote, Open Document.
  • ● Pre-written follow-up emails — approval chasers (standard and urgent) are pre-written and pre-addressed. Builder clicks "Send follow-up" and the email client opens with reference, days outstanding, and professional language already composed.
  • ● Summary bar — active project count, total contract value across all projects, and total items needing attention at a glance.
  • ● Project detail page — dedicated project.html with full project timeline, documents by type, contract summary, smart actions, and coming-soon stubs for Client Portal, Digital Signatures, Progress Claims, Invoices, Site Photos, Voice Notes, and Attachments.
  • ● Hero personalisation — dashboard greeting updates based on project health state: "2 projects need your attention today" vs "All 4 projects looking healthy."
Architecture notes
  • ● js/toolkit/health-engine.js — pure module, no DOM, no side effects. HEALTH_ENGINE_SWAP_POINT comment marks where evaluateProject() would be replaced with an AI API call.
  • ● js/toolkit/project-store.js — PROJECT_STORAGE_POINT marks the swap point for Supabase backend in v2.
  • ● js/toolkit/document-history.js — DOCUMENT_HISTORY_STORAGE_POINT marks the swap point for server-side document storage.
Version 0.9 Beta 15 July 2026 — First public beta

The first beta release of BIK Business Toolkit. Five professional document tools, AI writing assistance, and a complete document management workflow — built specifically for Australian builders and tradies. This release is ready for real-world use on Variation Notices. The remaining four tools are available and functional, with further polish planned before full launch.

New in this release
  • ● Variation Notice Generator — complete, production-quality tool. Auto-numbered VN references, cost and time extension calculations with GST, AI writing modes, PDF export, email workflow, draft autosave, and document history.
  • ● Quote Builder — dynamic line items pricing table, GST calculations, inclusions/exclusions, validity period, payment schedule, and AI writing assistance.
  • ● Scope of Works — comprehensive scope documentation with site details, materials, inclusions, exclusions, authority requirements, and contract conditions.
  • ● Site Diary — daily site records including weather, workforce, works completed, deliveries, incidents, inspections, and sub-contractor notes.
  • ● Defect Report — structured defect identification with severity levels, location, access requirements, trade responsible, and rectification instructions.
  • ● AI Writer — six writing modes: Rewrite Professionally, Strengthen for Contract, Spell & Grammar, Simplify for Client, Formal Version, Plain English. Uses your own Anthropic API key.
  • ● Builder Profile — set once, auto-fills across all documents. Business name, ABN, licence, phone, email, address, and authorised representative.
  • ● Document History — all generated documents saved automatically with full load/duplicate/delete functionality.
  • ● Draft Autosave — forms autosave every 1.2 seconds. Drafts survive browser refreshes and can be restored on next visit.
  • ● Beta feedback widget — leave a bug report, idea, feature request, or rating from the banner on every page.
  • ● Trust documents — Privacy Policy, Terms of Use, AI Usage Notice, Support, and Release Notes now published.
Known issues in 0.9 Beta
Known issues
  • PDF print quality may vary between browsers. Chrome and Edge produce the best results. On Safari, use File → Print for best layout.
  • Quote Builder line items may occasionally wrap awkwardly across PDF page breaks on very long quotes. Workaround: use Edit document mode to adjust spacing before printing.
  • Defect severity badge colours may not print on some printers with colour printing disabled. Check print preview before sending.
  • Document history is limited to this browser and device. There is no cloud sync in Version 0.9.
